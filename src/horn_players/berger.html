Hans Berger

Biography

Hans Berger (born in 1907 and died in 1996) was a horn player.

Berger studied in Vienna with the Karl Stiegler, then first horn of the Vienna Philharmonic. From 1935 to 1941 he holds the position of principal horn of the Berlin Philharmonic, as successor to Max Zimolong.

In 1941 he moves to the solo horn position of the Vienna Philharmonic, along with Gottfried von Freiberg. His colleagues in the Vienna Philharmonic, around 1961, were:

  • Gottfried von Freiberg
  • J. Veleba
  • O. Nitsch
  • Jos. Koller
  • E. Kreuziger
  • W. Tomböck Senior
  • Roland Berger
  • Josef Lackner
  • Josef Kolle

His son Roland (born in 1937 in Berlin), whom he taught, would later become principal horn of the Vienna Philharmonic.
