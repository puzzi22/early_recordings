The First Horn Players To Play For Future Ears

The end of the 19th century saw the invention of recorded sound. Horn players, like pianists and singers beforehand, started to play on recordings, first as part of orchestras, but soon also as soloist and as part of chamber music ensembles. This website aims to present some of these recordings, highlighting the great stylistical changes that occured in the past 120 years.
