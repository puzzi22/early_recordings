Copyrights

Unless specified explicitally, all material used on this website is in the public domain, meaning they are no longer protected by copyright and can be used freely. As a matter of fact, in the European Union, a piece whose composer died more than 70 years ago automatically pertains to the public domain.

Sound recordings also have copyrights. In the European Union, 70 years after the release, a recording falls to public domain. The law applies to all recordings first issued November 1, 1963 and later. This is the reason why most classical music recordings from the period 1900-1950 can be freely distributed, and available online on platforms like YouTube, from which most sound samples on this website are derived.
